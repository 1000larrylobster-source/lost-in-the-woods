# Lost in the Woods — Story Line (complete)

Card conventions: every card is 1–3 short lines, shown as quiet text over the world, no VO. Mid-map beats fire on the trigger noted in italics. The forest does the talking; cards just point at it.

---

## 1. Title screen

> Dusk. No pack, no trail, no name for these woods yet.
> Everyone who lives out here got here the same way: lost.
> Start walking.

---

## 2. The ten maps

Each map is a region of the Range. Mechanic anchors match BACKLOG.md's build order.

### Map 1 — THE VALLEY (siting, coverage, first fire, first rescue)

**Opening card**
> Dusk. You don't know these woods.
> They don't know you yet, either.
> There's a light down the hill.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first smoke detection (or first eye-spotted smoke, if sited badly)*
> There. One tree.
> It's only a canyon fire if you're late.

**Closing card** — *after the night rescue: walking Dale home*
> The forest remembers.
> So do you — what it is to be found.
> Now you're the one holding the light.

### Map 2 — THE FORK (component assembly, hauling)

**Opening card**
> Across the river, the map goes quiet.
> Towers don't come in boxes out here.
> They come in pieces, where they fell.

**Mid-map beat** — *on hauling the fourth component to the pad*
> Base, mast, antenna, panel. Four parts, four trips.
> The mountain charges by the pound.

**Closing card** — *Dale arrives at the finished tower*
> The man you walked home came back.
> Gloves on. Took the heavy end.
> The first one who stayed.

### Map 3 — THE SADDLE (relay mesh, antenna aim)

**Opening card**
> Two valleys watched. The Range past them, dark.
> A tower alone talks to no one.
> Get high. Aim true.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first tower-to-tower link*
> Ping. Then the answer-ping.
> Two towers, one conversation.
> That's a network — the smallest one there is.

**Closing card** — *Mae rescued near the old repeater site*
> Mae came up chasing a repeater she built in '09.
> Found yours instead.
> She stays, to keep the links clean.

### Map 4 — THE OLD BURN (degradation & maintenance)

**Opening card**
> This region burned, years back. Before anyone watched.
> Black snags, ash soil, sun with nothing to stop it.
> Things wear out fast here.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first panel cleaned / component repaired*
> Ash on the panel by noon. Yield down by half.
> The network isn't built once.
> It's built every day.

**Closing card** — *Hollis found at the footings of the old lookout*
> Hollis staffed a lookout here, back when there was one.
> He missed the big one by a night's sleep.
> He doesn't sleep past dawn anymore. He stays.

### Map 5 — THE NORTH SLOPE (cold, shelter, sleep, night)

**Opening card**
> Cold country. The sun works at a slant here.
> Night comes early and takes the drones' eyes with it.
> Build shelter before you build anything else.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first forced sleep while a fire risk stands*
> You have to sleep. The fire doesn't.
> That's the whole problem with being one person.

**Closing card** — *Birdie rescued after dark*
> Birdie followed an owl past dusk, and the dark kept her.
> She hears everything first — wings, wind, the wrong crackle.
> Night watch, covered.

### Map 6 — THE MEADOWS (animals: hunting + false positives)

**Opening card**
> Open ground, tall grass, deer moving through like weather.
> The drones flag everything warm.
> Learning what not to fear is the work now.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first false positive confirmed on foot*
> Heat signature, moving. An hour's hike.
> A mule deer, staring back.
> A false alarm you answer beats a real one you don't.

**Closing card** — *June rescued in the berry ground*
> June was out mapping the berry ground her grandmother picked.
> She knows every plant by first name.
> The caches stay full now.

### Map 7 — THE DRY FORK (wind, fast spread)

**Opening card**
> A canyon that funnels wind like a bellows.
> Everything here is fuel, and the fuel is dry.
> Fires in the Fork don't walk. They run.

**Mid-map beat** — *on first wind-driven spread outrunning a straight cut*
> The wind arrow is the truest thing you own.
> Cut where the fire is going.
> Not where it is.

**Closing card** — *Cole found far up-canyon*
> Cole came up here to be nobody for a while. Bad year behind him.
> The canyon didn't care. You found him anyway.
> Fastest legs in the Range. He runs the parts now.

### Map 8 — THE GRANITE (storms, lightning ignitions, storm damage)

**Opening card**
> Above the treeline, the Range shows its bones.
> Storms come off the peaks and take antennas with them.
> Lightning starts more fires than people do.

**Mid-map beat** — *after the first storm passes*
> The mast held. The antenna didn't.
> Climb. Re-rig. The mesh is only as strong
> as its worst joint.

**Closing card** — *Teo rescued below a strike-scarred spur*
> Teo was up here photographing the lightning that nearly got him.
> He loves weather too much to fear it.
> Now he climbs the masts when it's coming in.

### Map 9 — THE FAR REACH (everything combined; the mesh closes)

**Opening card**
> The last dark corner of the map.
> Old search posters, sun-bleached, still stapled to the trailhead board.
> People used to get lost out here and stay lost.

**Mid-map beat** — *on the final relay linking back to Tower One*
> The last relay finds the first tower, nine regions back.
> Every tower answers every tower.
> The Range is one animal now, and it's awake.

**Closing card** — *Wren, after the drones find the old camp*
> Wren came looking for a brother the searches never found.
> The drones found his last camp on the third sweep. An answer, finally.
> She stays. Nobody stays lost out here now.

### Map 10 — FIRE SEASON (the finale — the whole Range, one season)

**Opening card**
> Forty days, no rain. The grass has gone the color of a struck match.
> Ten regions. Every tower. Everyone you ever walked home.
> This is what all of it was for.

*(Mid-map beats and closing are the finale sequence — section 4.)*

## 4. The finale sequence (Map 10 beats)

1. **Roll call.** Season opens. The radio pings tower by tower, each recruit one line, their voice. Last check-in: Tower One, the Valley, where you woke.
   > All towers answering.
   > Say when.

2. **Dry lightning.** A strike night — multiple ignitions across regions at once. The network catches most while they're one tree; Watchers fly their sectors, you triage by radio and boots. This is every map's lesson replayed at speed.

3. **The gap.** One fire starts where nothing was watching — a panel you didn't tend, an antenna the storm took, a corner you always meant to cover. The honest lesson, one card:
   > It didn't start where the network was weak.
   > It started where you were.

4. **Everyone works.** The fire becomes a front. Scripted assists, one per recruit, each their role at its best: Cole runs a battery to a dead tower ahead of you; Mae reroutes the mesh around a burned relay; Hollis calls the wind shift an hour early; Birdie hears it turn at 3 a.m.; June keeps the line fed; Teo climbs a mast in smoke; Dale hauls the break-line gear; Wren pulls two trapped hikers out ahead of the front. No card longer than one line each.

5. **The hold.** The final firebreak — the whole network converging on one cut, placed where the wind says, held at night, tended mid-fight. Everything from maps 1–9, one line.
   > One tree, once.
   > Hold.

6. **After.** Rain, finally. Dawn on the burn line — green where you weren't late, scars where you were, and both stay in the save. Walk it. The radio pings, tower to tower, all quiet. Then the closing card.

## 5. Closing card of the game

> The rain came the way it always does — late.
> Count the towers. Count the people. Everyone up there was lost once.
> The lost don't leave the woods. They become its watchers.

**Post-credits card** (optional, the real-hardware wink — cut if it breaks the spell):

> The towers are real. A Pi, a camera, a panel, a LoRa whip.
> Build one. Watch something.
