# devlog 006 — the night everything became real

One session. Four systems. The game stopped being a demo tonight.

**Towers are built by hand now.** No more magic button: the BASE, the SCAFFOLD, the
ANTENNA, and the SOLAR PANEL are out there where they fell — a crashed supply drop, a
ruined ranger station, a lightning-felled mast — and you carry them to your chosen pad
one at a time, on your back, slower and breathing harder. Mount them in the order
physics demands. The tower rises piece by piece, and when the last panel bolts on, the
LED blinks amber: it's a body without eyes. The drone is its own build — frame, rotors,
battery, camera — and only when it spins up does the first watch begin. You can set a
part down anywhere and come back; the forest holds it for you.

**The node has real physics.** Panels foul over days and cut yield. Batteries charge by
sun and spend by watch — a drone in the air is power leaving. Run dry and the station
goes dark: the drone sets itself down, detection dies, the LED blinks red over a valley
nobody's watching. Rotors wear; worn drones limp. And the fixes are your hands: wipe
the panel, true the rotors. This is the part of the game that is secretly a Raspberry
Pi on a bench — every failure mode in here is one the real node has.

**The first neighbor.** Walk the lost hiker home and at first light he comes back with
a pack and a tarp. DALE settles by your tower — quiet, embarrassed about the one wrong
fork he took, strong back. Talk to him by the fire. And overnight, he works: wake to
find a scaffold he hauled sitting by your half-built pad. Every hiker you save from
here on is a neighbor you haven't met yet. That's the game's whole heart, and it works.

**Nights are cold now.** Warmth is the fifth vital. The dark chills you, the river
soaks you, a campfire gives it back fast. And when you bed down at a camp — the one
mercy the game offers — dawn comes in a breath *for you.* The forest keeps its own
time: the suite's most honest test ignites a fire, sleeps through the night, and
asserts the burn GREW while you dreamed. It did.

Suite: 32 → 44 on-canvas tests across the night, every one green in a real browser.
Under it all: the SHIPPED spec is locked (DESIGN.md v2 — ten maps, the works), the full
design pack is in `design/` (map progression, story, sound recipes, easter eggs, the
paid-quality bar), and the architecture for the Range is next.

The plan from here: the Range. Ten regions, one year, relay towers aimed at country
you haven't seen yet.
